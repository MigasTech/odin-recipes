Cozido à Portuguesa

Description of the recipe

Cozido is a classic Portuguese stew made with various types and cuts of meat and different vegetables. It appears in numerous frugal and sophisticated variations throughout the country and can also incorporate smoked meat and spicy smoked or blood sausages. This comforting winter meal is believed to have originated as a simple farmer’s dish which soon became a restaurant staple and a part of Portuguese national cuisine. Traditionally, the cooked ingredients are served on a platter accompanied by rice and beans, while the flavorful broth is usually enjoyed as an appetizer.

List of the ingredients

  • Savoy cabbage
  • Turnip
  • Carrot
  • Potatoes
  • Beef
  • Portuguese chouriço
  • Farinheira (Portuguese smoked sausage)
  • Chispe (pig paw)
  • Pig's ear
  • Blood sausage
  • Rice
  • Salt

Steps

  1. In a pressure cooker, add the beef, chispe (pig paw), pig’s ear, Portuguese chouriço and water. Season with salt and simmer for about 30 minutes.
  2. When everything is cooked, remove to a plate and reserve the cooking water.
  3. In a saucepan, add the farinheira (Portuguese smoked sausage) and the blood sausage. Boil over high heat. When starts boiling, reduce to low heat and cook about 7 to 8 minutes. Turn off the heat, drain and set aside.
  4. If you want to prepare rice with the stew: Remove a little of the cooking water into a saucepan and boil over high heat. When starts boiling, reduce to low heat, add the rice and season with a little salt. Stir and cook for about 10 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, peel and wash the potatoes. Place the potatoes in a pan with the remaining cooking water, the savoy cabbage cut into pieces, the carrots cut into strips and the turnip. Boil over high heat. When starts boiling, reduce to medium-low heat and cook about 25 minutes.
    6. Last but not least turn off the heat and serve! :)
